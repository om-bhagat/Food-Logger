Most people can’t answer a simple question: “Which meals make me feel good, and which ones mess me up?” Memory is unreliable. Symptoms lag. Advice online is loud but not specific to you. I wanted a tiny tool that connects what I ate to how I felt—quickly, privately, and without turning meals into homework.

The Problem (no sugar-coating)

  • Recall is trash. We remember drama, not patterns.
  • Latency is real. Evening bloating can be lunch’s fault.
  • Tracking apps are bloated. Ten taps to log a banana? Hard pass.
  • “Calories = truth” is narrow. The outcome matters more than the macro math.
If logging takes more than 30 seconds, no one sticks with it. So this app refuses to be heavy.

The Promise

Keep a ruthless, simple log—food, serving size, ingredients, notes, and a blunt outcome: Great / Meh / Not great. In a few weeks you’ll see patterns you can act on.

Fast

One form, keyboard shortcut, “Same as last,” ingredients as quick chips.

Portable

Works in any browser. Export JSON/CSV. No vendor lock-in.

Private by default

Local first. Sign in only if you want cross-device sync.

Design Principles

  • Outcome over obsession. Track results, not perfection.
  • Friction is the enemy. Every extra field kills the habit.
  • Clarity beats cleverness. Simple list. Simple stats. Zero noise.
  • Data is yours. You can export, import, or delete everything.

Technical Choices (and why)

Frontend

Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS for reliability and speed. Works offline with LocalStorage.

Sync

Optional Google sign-in + Firestore. Each user writes only to their own users/{uid}/entries via strict security rules.

Hosting

Vercel for simple, fast deploys. No server to babysit.

Portability

Exports in JSON/CSV so you can analyze in your own tools or share with a coach.

Privacy & Ownership

  • Local by default: your browser stores your log.
  • Opt-in sync with Google: convenient, not required.
  • Export everything. Delete everything. Your call.
  • No third-party trackers. No creepy analytics.

Non-Goals

Diet dogma

No prescriptive meal plans. This is a personal research tool.

Dashboard bloat

No 20-chart circus. You get the stats you’ll actually use.

My Personal Why

I write code for a living and I practice awareness off the keyboard. This project merges both: observe reality, then change it. A few honest logs beat a thousand opinions. If a small tool helps someone feel better next week than they did last week, that’s worth building.
